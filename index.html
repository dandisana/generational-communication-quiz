What each style means at work
Baby Boomer Born 1946–1964
Default: Chunk Down

You communicate through track record, experience, and demonstrated specifics. You build authority by showing what you've done. You distrust abstract vision without evidence, and you earn credibility through concrete detail.

"What have you actually done?"

Generation X Born 1965–1980
Default: Lateral / Pragmatic

You are sceptical of both grand vision and excessive detail. You want the logic, the trade-offs, the honest assessment. You find comparisons and precedents persuasive — show you what's worked elsewhere and you'll engage. You are often the most versatile communicator in the room.

"Does this actually make sense?"

Millennial Born 1981–1996
Default: Chunk Up

You lead with purpose, meaning, and values. You need to know why something matters before you can engage with what it is. You respond strongly to vision and narrative, and you struggle with communications that are purely operational or transactional.

"Why should I care?"

Generation Z Born 1997–2012
Default: Chunk Sideways

You think in comparisons, analogies, and cross-domain references. You process information laterally — like a network rather than a hierarchy. You shift between levels quickly and expect others to keep up. You are highly attuned to what's happening elsewhere.
